Interviews

About the snippet

We chose this snippet because it shed's light on an important research question we like to answer: Do we actually need an electronic learning environment. Let's say we do not. How will teachers then be able to arrange their courses? The answer we got was somewhat unexpected. The interviewee did not think the UvA would decide not to replace Blackboard by another electronic learning environment. This snippet also shows the actions taken by the interviewer to get an answer to the original question.

Reflection

The interviews we conducted went a lot better than last week. We feel that our questions were less biased than last week. We also talked louder, which invited the interviewee to talk louder as well resulting in better video footage.
When we prepared our questions we were too focused on the interview technique and applying positive cues that stimulate the interviewee to provide information. But because of this focus we lost sight of the actual goal, so we had some good interviews where the interviewee gave a lot of information but we did not get a lot of relevant information regarding the goals that we set.

Things to improve:

  • Focus on a specific goal.
  • Hasty reassurances/interruptions.
  • Talking in the abstract.
  • Specifically going about to find teachers to interview took a lot of time..

About

The contract for Blackboard comes to an end, so the UvA (University of Amsterdam) will need a new student-education system in the short future. Software Engineering master students Tom van Duist, and Kevin van den Bekerom will extract relevant requirements for the new system by means of several assignments, which can be read on this blog.
